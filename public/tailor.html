Tailoring

Tailoring is all about stitching up stylish clothes, from vibrant ankara gowns to sharp suits or even casual wears like joggers. For young Nigerians, this is one of the best handworks to learn in Nigeria because fashion is our heartbeat.

Whether it’s owambe season or Sunday service, people want to look fly, and they’ll pay for it. Imagine earning between N50,000 and N150,000 monthly just by mastering your sewing machine! It’s one of those vocational skills in Nigeria that works as a full-time hustle or a side gig if you’re still in school or juggling another job.

To get started, you’ll need a sewing machine (N80,000 to N300,000 depending on if you go manual or electric), scissors, measuring tape, threads, and a little creativity. Apprenticeships with a local tailor can take 6 months to 2 years, but if you’re tight on cash, YouTube has free tutorials to kick things off.

The beauty of tailoring? You can start small, maybe sewing for family, then grow to taking orders on Instagram. It’s a skill that pays daily in Nigeria once you build a name. Who doesn’t love a tailor who delivers on time?
