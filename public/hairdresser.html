Hairdressing and Barbing

Hairdressing for the ladies and barbing for the guys is a classic combo that never gets old. It’s hands-down one of the most profitable skills in Nigeria because hair is a daily need. Guys need a fresh fade every week, and ladies are always after braids, weaves, or wigs.

Barbers can pocket N5,000 to N15,000 daily, while hairdressers might pull in N50,000 to N100,000 monthly if they’re consistent. This is perfect as a side hustle or full-time gig, especially for young Nigerians looking for flexibility.

You’ll need clippers (N15,000) for barbing or a hairdryer and combs (N30,000 total) for hairdressing. Training takes 3 to 6 months at a salon near you, and trust me, once you’re good, customers will line up. It’s one of those skills that pay daily in Nigeria, especially if you’re in a busy area like Oshodi or Onitsha.

Start by practicing on friends, and before you know it, you’re the go-to person in your hood. Na small small you go turn big stylist!
