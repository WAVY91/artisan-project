Shoemaker

Shoemaking

Shoemaking, or crafting shoes by hand, is a hidden gem among handworks for young Nigerians. Nigerians love custom sandals, loafers, or even sneakers, and you can charge N10,000 to N30,000 per pair.

If you’re consistent, you could rake in N100,000 monthly, making it a solid side hustle or full-time gig. It’s perfect for creative minds who want to stand out.

You’ll need leather, glue, a last (shoe mold), and basic tools, costing around N50,000 to start. Learn from a shoemaker in 6 to 12 months or check online courses if you’re near a good Wi-Fi spot.

This is one of the best handworks to learn in Nigeria because you can sell at markets or online, and it’s a skill that pays daily in Nigeria once you get regular customers. Start with simple slippers, and soon you’ll be the next big name in footwear.
